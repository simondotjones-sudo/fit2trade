Opening statement

At Fit2Trade we know you care about how your personal information is used and shared, and we take your privacy seriously. Please read the following to learn more about how we collect, store, use and disclose information about you when you interact with or use our websites (collectively the "Websites") or any related events, sales or marketing, and/or if you use any of our products, services or applications (collectively the "Services").

For the purpose of the Data Protection Act 1998 and the GDPR as of 25 May 2018, the data controller is Fit2Trade Ltd, whose registered office is at The Masonry, Hanover Quay, Dublin 2, D02 VY79, Ireland.

What information do we collect?

When you use the Websites: We may collect any personal information that you choose to send to us or provide to us, for example, via an online form. If you contact us through the Websites, we will keep a record of our correspondence.

When you purchase the Services: We receive and store information you provide directly to us. For example, when setting up new users, we collect personal information such as name and email address. The types of information we may collect include: names, email addresses, work location, and any other contact or information uploaded to our systems in connection with the Services.

Information we automatically collect

When you use the Websites: When you visit our Websites, we collect certain information related to your device, such as your device's IP address, referring website, what pages your device visited, and the time of your visit.

When you use the Services: We keep track of user activity in relation to the types of Services used, the configuration of computers, and performance metrics. We log information such as IP addresses and use cookies and other similar technologies to collect information about your interaction with our Services. Please see our Cookie Policy for more detail.

How do we use the information?

We use the information we collect via our Websites:

  • To administer our Website and for internal operations, including troubleshooting, data analysis, testing, and statistical purposes
  • To improve our Website to ensure that content is presented in the most effective manner for you
  • To analyse customers' use of the Websites for trend monitoring and marketing purposes
  • For purposes made clear to you at the time you submit your information — for example, to fulfil a demo request or provide information you have requested
  • As part of our efforts to keep our Website safe and secure

We may use information we collect from customers and their users in connection with the Services to: set up user accounts; provide, operate and maintain the Services; process and complete transactions; manage customer enquiries; send technical alerts and administrative communications; and investigate and prevent fraudulent activities or unauthorised access.

We may also use your information to communicate with you about products, services and promotions we think may be of interest to you, where this is in accordance with your marketing preferences. You can opt out of such communications at any time.

How do we share and disclose information?

We do not rent or sell your personal information to anyone. We may share and disclose information about our customers in the following limited circumstances:

Vendors and service providers: We may share your information with third-party vendors and service providers who perform tasks on our behalf — including payment processors, analytics companies (such as Google Analytics), CRM providers (such as PipeDrive), and email service providers. These parties have no right to use the personal information we share with them beyond what is necessary to assist us.

Business transfers: If the Company (or its assets) is acquired, or if we go out of business, enter bankruptcy, or go through some other change of control, personal information could be one of the assets transferred to or acquired by a third party.

Legal compliance: We reserve the right to access, read, preserve, and disclose any information as necessary to comply with law or court order, enforce our agreements, or protect the rights, property, or safety of the Company, our employees, our users, or others.

Is personal information about me secure?

We use appropriate technical, organisational and administrative security measures to protect any information we hold from loss, misuse, and unauthorised access, disclosure, alteration and destruction. Unfortunately, no company or service can guarantee complete security.

Your account is protected by a password for your privacy and security. You must prevent unauthorised access to your account and personal information by selecting and protecting your password appropriately and limiting access to your browser by signing off after you have finished accessing your account.

Cookies and other tracking technologies

Our Websites and some of our Services use cookies and other similar tracking technologies (like web beacons, tags and scripts) to uniquely identify your browser and to gather information about how you interact with the Websites and Services. We use this information to assist in navigation, to enable login and feedback functionality, and to analyse use of our products and services.

We also utilise Google Analytics, a web analysis service provided by Google, to better understand your use of our Websites. Please consult our Cookie Policy for full information about the types of cookies we use and how to accept or reject them.

Your privacy rights

Under the General Data Protection Regulation, you have the following rights:

  • Right to be forgotten — if you ask us, we will erase all data we hold about you
  • Right to be informed — this privacy policy explains what information we collect and how we use it
  • Right of access — you have the right to request all of your personal data that we hold
  • Right to object — you have the right to object and be removed from any direct marketing emails

To exercise any of these rights, please contact our Data Protection Officer at info@fit2trade.com.

You can also opt out of receiving promotional communications from us at any time by using the unsubscribe link in any email communication we send.

Where we keep your data

Our learning portal is hosted on an encrypted database operated by Microsoft Azure. The database is located in Ireland. The Microsoft Azure secure system is ISO/IEC 27018 Certified.

The Customer Service team and your Account Manager can view your account to assist with any queries. They can only see your name, email address and course progress. The IT Manager and the Data Protection Officer can also see your data.

If you have been given access to a course by your Company, the Administrator of your Company's learning account will be able to see your name, email address, and course progress (including completion status, progress percentage, and assessment scores).

Contact the DPO

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy or how we handle your personal information, please contact our Data Protection Officer, Tom Kinsella, at info@fit2trade.com.

You may also write to us at: Fit2Trade Ltd, The Masonry, Hanover Quay, Dublin 2, D02 VY79, Ireland.
