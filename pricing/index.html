Pricing questions

Common questions

What exactly counts as a “location”?+

A location is any physical address where your business operates — a store, restaurant, care home, childcare setting, credit union branch or office. If you operate from a single premises, that’s one location. A chain of 15 supermarkets is 15 locations. All employees assigned to a location are included in that location’s subscription — there’s no per-seat cap.

How does the employee count work for new starters?+

Your employee count is your typical steady-state headcount. If someone leaves and is replaced by a new starter, the replacement doesn’t add to your bill. If you grow your headcount, pricing adjusts at renewal. We don’t charge per training completion or per course — the per-employee rate covers unlimited training within your chosen products.

Is the 10% annual discount applied to the whole bill?+

Yes — the 10% discount applies to your total monthly cost multiplied by 12, giving you one reduced annual figure. For example, if your monthly cost is €500, your annual price would be €500 × 12 = €6,000, minus 10% = €5,400. That’s €600 saved each year.

Are there setup fees or minimum contract lengths?+

No setup fees. Your account is configured and templates are loaded by our team as part of onboarding — included in your subscription. For monthly billing, you can cancel with 30 days’ notice. For annual billing, you commit for 12 months and can adjust products at renewal. There’s no minimum term beyond your chosen billing cycle.

Do you offer volume discounts for large networks?+

Yes — volume pricing applies for 10 or more locations. The per-location rate reduces as your network grows. Exact discounts are confirmed during your demo once we know your site count and product mix. Franchise networks and large multi-site operators typically benefit significantly from volume pricing.

Can I start with one product and add others later?+

Yes — every product can be added to your subscription at any point. Most customers start with Ensure alone, add Educate within the first few months, and build out from there. Adding a product mid-contract is pro-rated to your next billing cycle, so you’re never paying for something before you use it.
