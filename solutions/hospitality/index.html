Allergen & Food Safety Training

Allergen liability is your biggest food safety risk. We eliminate it.

Under Natasha's Law and EU Regulation 1169/2011, you must be able to prove that every food handler on your premises has completed allergen awareness training — including agency workers and casual staff who may have only just joined. Fit2Trade makes this automatic.

  • Allergen awareness course completed online before first shift
  • Covers all 14 major allergens and cross-contamination risks
  • Certificate issued on completion — stored against each employee
  • Expiry alerts ensure recertification is never missed

Common questions

Questions from hospitality managers

Does Fit2Trade cover HACCP requirements under EC Regulation 852/2004?+

Yes — Ensure's food safety module is designed to satisfy EC Regulation 852/2004 which requires all food businesses to implement and maintain HACCP-based food safety management systems. Your digital records include all the evidence an EHO or FSAI inspector requires — CCPs, monitoring logs, corrective actions and verification procedures.

Can I prove allergen training for agency workers who worked a single shift?+

Yes. Agency workers are added to your Fit2Trade portal before their first shift and must complete the same allergen awareness training as permanent staff. Their training certificate is stored against their profile and remains accessible to you even after their shift is complete — so you have a permanent record regardless of staff turnover.

What if we get an improvement notice from the EHO — can Fit2Trade help?+

Absolutely. Improvement notices typically require evidence that specific corrective actions have been taken within a defined period. Fit2Trade's corrective action management module lets you assign, track and close each action, with a complete audit trail showing dates, responsible parties and completion evidence. This is exactly what inspectors need to see at a re-visit.

We have a hotel with a restaurant, bar and spa — can Fit2Trade cover all departments?+

Yes — each area or department within a property can have its own set of routines, checklists and risk assessments. Staff are assigned to their department and see only the tasks relevant to them. Your head of compliance gets a single view across all departments from one dashboard.
