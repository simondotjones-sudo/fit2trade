Common questions

Questions from credit union managers

Is the training content ILCU-accredited?+

Yes — Fit2Trade's compliance training for credit unions is ILCU-accredited, meaning it is accepted for CBI audit purposes as evidence of staff training completion. Our courses are reviewed annually to reflect any regulatory updates from the Central Bank of Ireland.

How does the MCC tracker work?+

The MCC tracker shows the qualification status of every employee across all 8 MCC product areas. When a staff member obtains a CBI-recognised qualification (e.g. QFA, APA, CIP), it is recorded against their profile in the system. The dashboard shows which employees have gaps, which qualifications are nearing expiry, and provides a complete evidence pack for any CBI supervisory meeting. The October 2028 deadline is tracked automatically.

We have multiple branches — can we manage them all from one portal?+

Yes — the CU Complete tier is designed for multi-branch credit unions. Each branch has its own operational checklists, safety routines and incident records, while head office sees a consolidated view across all locations. Training and MCC data is aggregated at portfolio level for board and management reporting.

Is DORA already mandatory for credit unions?+

DORA introduces obligations for financial entities regulated by the CBI, including credit unions. While the CBI is still clarifying the precise timeline and scope for credit unions, it has signalled clearly that ICT resilience is a supervisory priority. Our DORA packs help you get ahead of requirements without over-investing before full requirements are confirmed — and they provide the CBI with evidence of proactive governance.

Can we start with one tier and upgrade later?+

Yes — the tiers are additive. Start with CU Essential (training and MCC tracking), add Ensure (H&S management) when you're ready to go fully digital on safety, and add Operate (operational checklists) whenever it makes sense. There's no lock-in — you upgrade at your annual renewal.
